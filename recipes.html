Soups

Miso Soup

A classic start to any Japanese meal with umami-rich stock.

5-10 min Dairy Vegan Savory
Ingredients

• 1 cup of water

• 2 teaspoons Dashi granules

• 3 tablespoons Miso Paste

• 1 package of Tofu

• 1 package of Seaweed

• Green Onions (Optional)

Steps

1. In a pot, combine water and dashi over medium-high heat until boil.

2. Reduce to medium head and whisk in miso paste.

3. Add in tofu and seaweed and simmer for 2-3 minutes

4. Remove heat and serve in a bowl. Optional to top with green onions.


Tonjiru

Nourishing soup best served in the winter

20-30 min Savory Gluten
Ingredients

For Soup Ingredients:

    • 1/2 lb Pork Belly

    • 1 Burdock Root

    • 1 Taro

    • 1 Konjac

    • 1 package of Tofu

For Soup:

    • 1 cup of Dashi Stock

    • 1 tablespoon Miso Paste

    • 3/4 teaspoon Sesame Oil

Steps

1. Cut the pork belly into 1" cube sizes.

2. Scrape the outer skin of the burdok root with a knife, do not use a peeler. After that, make a cross incision and shave off slice of the root. Rinse them well and set aside

3. Peel the taro with a knife and cut into 1/3" cube sizes. Soak well and set aside.

4. Slice radish and carrots into thin slices and set aside.

5. Cut the konjac into rectangular pieces and rub the pieces with salt; let it sit for 5 minutes. Boil the kanjac pieces for 2-3 minutes and drain.

6. In a pot, stir-fry the porkbelly slices with cooking oil until cooked. Add the radish, carrots, burdock root, taro, and konjac after that.

7. Pour in the dashi stock and and cover it with a lid until it's boiling. After that, lower heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes.

8. Turn off heat. With a small strainer, pour the miso paste and whisk into the soup. Top with sesame oil and serve in a bowl.


Udon Soup

Thick or thin chewy noodles with a simple broth, enjoyed as it is.

Savory Gluten Vegan
Ingredients

• 1 serving of udon noodles

• 1 cup of Dashi Stock

• 3/4 tablespoons Soy Sauce

• 1/2 tablespoons Mirin (Sweet Cooking Wine)

• 1/2 teaspoon sugar

• Pre-Made Udon Stock

Steps

1. Cook noodles in boiling water for 5-8 minutes, or until fully cooked

2. In another pot, pour soy sauce, dashi stock, mirin, and sugar together and bring it to a simmer. For pre-made udon broth, follow instructions on the package.

3. Put udon and broth in a bowl and serve. You can also add additional toppings, like seaweed, fish cakes, fried tofu, and more.


Sukiyaki Mini Hot Pot

Sweet and salty broth with nutritious ingredients in rich with flavor for one serving

15-20 min Gluten Sweet and Salty
Ingredients

• 1 cup of Dashi Kombu Stock

• 1/4 cup Sake

• 1/4 cup Mirin (Sweet Cooking Wine)

• 1 1/2 tablespoons Sugar

• 1/4 cup Soy Sauce

• Hot Pot Ingredients:

    Protein: Thin Slices of Chicken, Beef, Pork; Tofu; Egg

    Vegetables: Napa Cabbage, Bok Choy, Carrots, Mushroom (Enoki, Shitake, Maitake)

    Noodles: Ramen or Udon, Thin or Thick

Steps

1. In a saucepan, pour soy sauce, sake, mirin, and sugar together and mix over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved or mix comes to a boil.

2. Prepare all Hot Pot Ingredients to your liking. This includes cutting the proteins and vegetables to peeling any excess dirt

3. Put udon and broth in a bowl and serve. You can also add additional toppings, like seaweed, fish cakes, fried tofu, and more.


Side Dishes

Steamed Vegetables

Simple side of vegetable softened for the tongue and seasoned lightly, in less than 3 minutes.

2-3 min Vegan Dairy-Free Gluten-Free
Ingredients

• Assorted Vegetables (Carrots, Mushrooms, Broccoli, etc.)

• 2 teaspoons butter(optional)

• Salt

Steps

1. Cut vegetables to a desired size and amount and put in a bowl.

2. Add a splash of watter, butter, and salt.

3. Cover with a microavable plastic wrap and microwave for 2-3 minutes on high power.

4. Carefully remove the plastic wrap (be careful of steam), mix vegetables and enjoy.


Tempura

Vegetables, shrimp, and more, dipped a light batter and fried to perfection.

10-15 min Gluten Vegan Contains Egg Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 1 cup of cold water

• 1 Egg

• 1 cup of all-purpose flour (Optional to change to gluten-free)

• Any seafood or vegetable

    * Recommended vegetables to fry: broccoli, mushrooms, squash, sweet potato, and carrots.

    * Recommended seafood to fry: shrimp, white fish chunks

Steps

1. Beat egg thoroughly.

2. In a bowl, mix flour (sifted if possible), beaten egg, and water together. For a crispy result, use ice-cold water.

3. In a deep-fryer or pot of vegetable oil, heat up to 355F-350F (170C-180C). Dip your seafood or vegetable in the batter and cook until golden (3-5 minutes).

4. Remove from fryer and place on a paper towel-covered plate to drain excel oil. Can be served with rice or sauce.


Rolled Omelet

Whisked egg cooked and rolled into a beautiful rectangular shape

5-10 min Eggs
Ingredients

• 2 Eggs

• 1/4 teaspoon sugar

• 1/8 teaspoon salt

• 1/8 teaspoon Dashi granules

• 1/8 teaspoon soy sauce

• 4 tablespoons water

Steps

1. In a small bowl or cup, mix sugar, salt, Dashi granules, soy sauce together. Add water and mix until sugar is dissolved.

2. Whisk eggs in another bowl to a good consistency. Add in the dashi mixture, and mix.

3. Heat up pan to a medium high heat with olive oil. There are two ways to cook depending on pan:

  • (1) Round pan - Pour in egg mixture. Once it looks like its alsmost cooked, fold the sides so shape is recangular.
    Roll egg mixture from the top side with a spatula or chopsticks and push it to the side of the pan farthest from you. Repeat this step until there is no leftover egg mixture.

  • (2) Square pan - Pour in egg mixture. Once it looks like its alsmost cooked, roll egg mixture from the top side with a spatula or chopsticks and push it to the side of the pan farthest from you. Repeat this step until there is no leftover egg mixture.

4. Place on a plate and cut it into slices to enjoy.


Fried Tofu

A light delicacy that can simply be enjoyed with just soy sauce.

10-15 min Vegan Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 1 block of Medium-Firm Tofu

• 4 tablspoons potato starch/cornstarch

• Cooking Oil

Steps

1. Heat oil in pot or fryer up to 330F (165C).

2. Cut tofu block into bite size pieces and coat in cornstarch evenly.

3. Add three pieces at a time in the fryer and cook until light brown or crispy (2-3 minutes).

4. Drain excess oil from fried pieces with a paper-towel tray and enjoy. Best served with soy sauce on the side.


Potato Salad

A potato salad recipe that brings out the sweetness in vegetables and its seasonings.

10-15 min Vegan Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 2 Potatoes

• Mayonnaise

• 1/4 cup of canned/frozen corn

• One Carrot

• 2 slices of ham

• Salt

Steps

1. Cut potatoes to 1 1/2 pieces and boil them in a pot with a pinch of salt over medium high heat for 15 minutes.

2. Drain water and mash potatoes to a good consistency.

3. In another pot, boil corn for 5 minutes, and boil egg to hard boil for 8-11 minutes. Peel egg and cut into small pieces

4. Slice carrots and ham into thin pieces.

5. Mix all ingredients together with mayonnaise and enjoy.

Curry Rice
Comfort Food 30 min Gluten Vegan Dairy Free
Ingredients

• One Carrot

• One Mushroom

• One Potato

• 1/2 lb Meat (Optional)

• 1 1/2 pieces of Curry Roux Mix

Steps

1. Chop vegetables and meat and stir fry them in a pot until cooked

2. Pour 1 1/4 cups of water and wait for it to boil. After that, turn to low heat and cover pot. Let it simmer for 15-20 min on low heat.

3. Remove heat and add curry roux in to the mix by pieces.

4. Pour water amount indicated by the curry roux’s box and let it simmer for 15-20 min on low heat.

5. Serve with rice.


Yakisoba

Savory stiry-fry noodles filled with umami flavor!

10-20 min Vegan Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 1/2 lb of pork belly

• 1 Carrot

• 2 Mushroom

• 2 Cabbage Leaves

• 1/4 Onion

• Black Pepper

• Cooked Yakisoba noodles

• 1 Egg (Optional)

For Yakisoba Sauce (store-bough optional)

    • 4 tablespoons of worchestshire

    • 4 tablespoons of oyster sauce

    • 4 teaspoons of ketchu

    • 2 teaspoons of soy sauce

    • 2 teaspoons of sugar

Steps

1. Combine sauce ingredients in a bowl and set aside.

2. Slice carrot, mushroom, and onion into julienne strips. Cut cabbage and pork belly into bite-size pieces.

3. In a skillet or wok, cook pork belly on medium-high heat until well done, and add vegetables after. Cook for 1-2 minutes. Season with black pepper.

4. Lower heat and add yakisoba noodles. Pour yakisoba sauce and mix until it's well incorporated.

5. Optional Step is to cook an egg sunny side up. Place on top of noodles and serve on a plate.


Oyakodon

A chicken and egg rice bowl that melts in your mouth!

5 min Vegan Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 1/2 lb of chicken thighs

• 1/4 Onion

• 2-3 Eggs

• 1/2 cup of Dashi stock

• 2 tablespoons of soy sauce

• 2 tablespoons of sugar

• 2 tablespoons of mirin

• Short grain rice

Steps

1. In a saucepan, add Dashi, soy sauce, sugar, and mirin and simmer at medium heat.

2. Slice onions into thin strips and add to mix. Cook for 5 minutes or until transparent.

3. Cut chicken into bite-size pieces and add to mix. Cook thoroughly for 5 minutes until meat is no longer pink.

4. Beat eggs and slowly drizzle across the saucepan. Cook at a medium heat until eggs are cooked, or slightly runny depending on preference.

5. Use a spatula to scoop food and put over a bowl of rice.


Salmon Ochazuke

Crispy lightly-salted salmon with rice submerged in a savory tea-based broth

10-15 min Vegan Dairy-Free
Ingredients

• 1 slice of salmon

• Short grained rice

• 1/2 cup of brewed green tea

• 1/2 tablespoon Dashi granules

• 2 teaspoons of soy sauce

• 2 tablespoons of sugar

• Short grain rice

Steps

1. Season salmon with salt and cook over pan or oven for 7-8 minutes until cooked.

2. In a saucepan, add green tea, Dashi granules, soy sauce, and sugar. Mix unil sugar is dissolved. Simmer broth at low heat for 3-5 minutes.

3. Place salmon over rice (cutting it pieces is optional) in a medium-sized bowl.

4. Pour green tea broth in dish and enjoyed. Better tastes when adding seaweed or rice crisps.


Tonkatsu

Fried Pork Cutlet dipped with a savory sauce that can be enjoyed with noodles, rice, or just by itself.

10-15 min
Ingredients

• 1/2 lb of Pork Loin or Chop

• Panko breadcrumbs

• 1 eggs

• 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

• 2 tablespoons of sugar

• Shredded cabbage

• Short grain rice

For Sauce:

    • 4 1/2 tablespoon Worcestshire sauce

    • 2 tablespoons of sugar

    • 5 tablespoons of ketchup

    • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce

Steps

1. Slice pork into desired pieces and pound meat with a mallet. Season pork with salt and pepper evenly after.

2. Prepare three bowls: one to whisk an egg, one for all-purpose flour, and one for Panko breadcrumbs

3. Dredge pork in flour, dip it in egg, and coat with panko breadcrumbs in that order. Set it aside for 5-10 minutes while preparing fryer.

4. In a frying pot or deep fryer, gently put one piece of pork at 340F and cook until golder. Cool and drain excess oil in a paper-towel tray for 5 minutes.

5. Combine all sauce ingredients thoroughly. Serve tonkatsu with sauce and shredded cabbage or rice.
